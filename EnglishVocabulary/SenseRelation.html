Sense relation are the relations held between the words within the vocabulary.

Polysemy

Polysemy means that one single word has two or more senses at the same time. Monosemic words are rare, and are mainly scientific terms. Most words are polysemous. Approaches
  • Diachronic approach: the result of growth and development of the semantic structure of the word, from primary meaning to derived meanings.
  • Synchronic approach: the coexistence of various meanings of the same word in a historical period from central meaning to secondary meaning.
Patterns of development
  • Radiation: A semantic process in which the primary meaning stands at the centre and the secondary meanings proceed out of it in every direction like rays.
  • Concatenation: A semantic process in which the meaning of a word moves gradually away from its first sense by successive shifts until, in many cases, there is not a sign of connection between the sense that is finally developed and that is at the beginning.
